# Against the Gods: The Remarkable Story of Risk
**Author:** Peter L. Bernstein

## What This Book Is About
A sweeping history of humanity's efforts to understand and manage risk, from ancient gamblers to modern financial theory. Bernstein traces how thinkers like Pascal, Bernoulli, Gauss, Galton, and Markowitz transformed our understanding of probability, regression to the mean, diversification, and behavioral biases, arguing that the mastery of risk is what distinguishes modern times from the past.

## Key Topics
- History of probability and statistics
- Fibonacci, Pascal, and the birth of probability theory
- Normal distribution and regression to the mean
- Portfolio theory and diversification
- Behavioral economics and the failure of invariance
- Risk measurement in financial markets

## When To Reach For This Book
When you want a rich historical narrative that connects the intellectual origins of probability and risk to modern decision-making and financial markets.
