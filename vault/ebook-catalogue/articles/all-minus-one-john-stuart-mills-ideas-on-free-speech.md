# All Minus One: John Stuart Mill's Ideas on Free Speech Illustrated
**Author:** Jonathan Haidt & Richard V. Reeves

## What This Book Is About
An illustrated adaptation of Chapter 2 of John Stuart Mill's "On Liberty," focusing on the importance of free speech and viewpoint diversity. Haidt and Reeves present Mill's arguments against social conformity and the tyranny of prevailing opinion, connecting his Victorian-era insights to modern challenges around campus speech, social media mob dynamics, and the confirmation bias.

## Key Topics
- Mill's arguments for freedom of expression
- The tyranny of prevailing opinion vs. government censorship
- Confirmation bias and why we need opposing viewpoints
- Campus free speech challenges
- Social media and modern speech suppression
- The value of intellectual diversity

## When To Reach For This Book
When you need a concise, accessible primer on why free speech and viewpoint diversity matter, especially in educational or organizational settings.
