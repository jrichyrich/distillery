# Thinking in Systems: A Primer
**Author:** Donella H. Meadows

## What This Book Is About
Thinking in Systems is the definitive introduction to systems thinking by one of the field's pioneers. Donella Meadows explains how systems work -- from bathtubs and thermostats to ecosystems and economies -- revealing the feedback loops, delays, and nonlinear dynamics that govern complex behavior. The book culminates in her famous "leverage points" framework for intervening in systems effectively. Published posthumously, it remains the most accessible entry point to systems dynamics.

## Key Topics
- Stocks, flows, and feedback loops
- Why systems behave in surprising ways
- Leverage points for intervening in systems
- System archetypes and common traps
- Applying systems thinking to real-world problems

## When To Reach For This Book
When you want to understand how complex systems work and where to intervene for maximum impact.
