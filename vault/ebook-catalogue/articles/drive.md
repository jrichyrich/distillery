# Drive: The Surprising Truth About What Motivates Us
**Author:** Daniel H. Pink

## What This Book Is About
Pink draws on decades of behavioral science research to argue that the traditional carrot-and-stick approach to motivation is fundamentally flawed. He presents a new framework built on three pillars of intrinsic motivation: autonomy (the desire to direct our own lives), mastery (the urge to get better at something that matters), and purpose (the yearning to serve something larger than ourselves).

## Key Topics
- The failure of extrinsic rewards (Motivation 2.0)
- Autonomy, mastery, and purpose as intrinsic motivators
- Type I vs. Type X behavior patterns
- Practical applications for organizations and individuals
- The science of flow states and engagement

## When To Reach For This Book
When you need to rethink how you motivate a team, design incentive structures, or understand why traditional rewards often backfire.
