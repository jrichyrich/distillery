# Superforecasting
**Author:** Philip E. Tetlock and Dan Gardner

## What This Book Is About
Superforecasting presents the findings from the Good Judgment Project, a massive government-funded study that discovered ordinary people could outperform intelligence analysts at predicting world events. Tetlock identifies the habits and thinking patterns of these "superforecasters" and shows that good forecasting is a skill that can be learned. The book challenges the notion that the future is inherently unpredictable.

## Key Topics
- What separates superforecasters from the rest
- Probabilistic thinking and calibration
- The importance of updating beliefs with new evidence
- Foxes vs. hedgehogs in prediction
- How to reduce overconfidence and cognitive bias

## When To Reach For This Book
When you want to improve your ability to make predictions and navigate uncertainty with better judgment.
