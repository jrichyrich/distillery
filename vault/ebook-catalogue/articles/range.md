# Range: Why Generalists Triumph in a Specialized World
**Author:** David J. Epstein

## What This Book Is About
Range makes the case that in most fields, generalists -- not specialists -- are primed to excel. David Epstein draws on research from sports, music, science, business, and education to show that sampling widely, gaining diverse experiences, and thinking broadly leads to greater creativity and adaptability. The book challenges the "10,000 hours" narrative and argues that late starts and winding paths are often advantages, not liabilities.

## Key Topics
- Why breadth of experience outperforms early specialization in most domains
- The difference between "kind" and "wicked" learning environments
- How analogical thinking and cross-domain knowledge fuel innovation
- The value of late starts and career switching
- Why the best problem solvers draw from diverse fields
- Implications for education, parenting, and career planning

## When To Reach For This Book
When you want evidence-based reassurance that a broad, varied background is a strength, or when you are rethinking the value of specialization.
