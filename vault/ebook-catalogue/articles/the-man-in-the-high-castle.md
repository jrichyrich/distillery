# The Man in the High Castle
**Author:** Philip K. Dick

## What This Book Is About
A Hugo Award-winning alternate history novel set in a world where the Axis powers won World War II. The former United States is divided between Japanese and German occupation zones, and multiple characters navigate this fractured reality while a mysterious novel-within-a-novel imagines a world where the Allies won, blurring the lines between fiction and truth.

## Key Topics
- Alternate history: Axis victory in World War II
- Japanese and German cultural hegemony in America
- The nature of reality and fiction
- Resistance, identity, and cultural adaptation
- The I Ching as narrative and philosophical device

## When To Reach For This Book
When you want a thought-provoking, genre-defining alternate history that questions the nature of reality itself.
