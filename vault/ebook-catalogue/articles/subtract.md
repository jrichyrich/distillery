# Subtract
**Author:** Leidy Klotz

## What This Book Is About
Subtract reveals a fundamental flaw in how humans solve problems: we almost always default to adding -- more features, more rules, more stuff -- when subtracting would often produce better results. Leidy Klotz draws on research from behavioral science, engineering, and design to explain why our brains are biased toward addition and how we can learn to overcome this tendency. The book makes a compelling case that the best improvements often come from removing rather than adding.

## Key Topics
- The cognitive bias toward addition over subtraction
- Research on why humans overlook subtractive solutions
- Subtraction in design, engineering, and daily life
- How organizations and systems become needlessly complex
- Practical strategies for cultivating a subtractive mindset
- Historical and contemporary examples of powerful subtraction

## When To Reach For This Book
When you suspect that the best solution to your problem is to take something away rather than add something new.
