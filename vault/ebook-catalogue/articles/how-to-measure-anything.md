# How to Measure Anything
**Author:** Douglas W. Hubbard

## What This Book Is About
A practical guide arguing that anything can be measured, even so-called intangibles like customer satisfaction, technology ROI, and organizational flexibility. Hubbard introduces applied information economics and calibrated estimation techniques that allow decision-makers to quantify uncertainty and reduce risk using surprisingly simple statistical methods.

## Key Topics
- Why things seem immeasurable but are not
- Computing the value of information to prioritize what to measure
- Calibrated probability assessment and reducing uncertainty
- Measuring soft outcomes like satisfaction, quality, and risk
- Applied information economics for business decisions

## When To Reach For This Book
When you need to make a business case for something everyone says cannot be quantified.
