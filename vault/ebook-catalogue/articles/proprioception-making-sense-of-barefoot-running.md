# Proprioception: Making Sense of Barefoot Running
**Author:** Lee Saxby

## What This Book Is About
This short guide explains the science behind barefoot running, focusing on proprioception -- the body's sense of position and movement. Lee Saxby argues that modern cushioned running shoes diminish sensory feedback from the feet, leading to poor form and chronic injuries. The book covers the evolutionary design of the human foot, the role of proprioception in movement quality, and provides a step-by-step approach to reawakening natural running skill.

## Key Topics
- The evolutionary design of the human foot
- Proprioception as the sixth sense for movement
- How padded shoes reduce sensory feedback and cause injury
- Minimalist footwear principles
- Rebuilding barefoot running technique

## When To Reach For This Book
When you want to understand the science behind barefoot and minimalist running and begin transitioning away from traditional cushioned shoes.
