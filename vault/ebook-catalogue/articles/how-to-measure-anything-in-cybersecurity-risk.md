# How to Measure Anything in Cybersecurity Risk
**Author:** Douglas W. Hubbard, Richard Seiersen

## What This Book Is About
Hubbard and Seiersen apply the "How to Measure Anything" methodology to cybersecurity, arguing that cyber risk can and should be measured quantitatively rather than with subjective ordinal scales. The book introduces calibrated probability estimation, Monte Carlo simulation, and other analytical techniques to replace the vague risk matrices that dominate most security programs with defensible, data-informed measurements.

## Key Topics
- Why ordinal risk scales (high/medium/low) are worse than useless
- Calibrated probability estimation for cyber risks
- Monte Carlo simulation for risk modeling
- Decomposing complex risks into measurable components
- Building a quantitative cyber risk measurement program
- Bayesian reasoning and updating beliefs with evidence

## When To Reach For This Book
When you want to apply rigorous measurement techniques to cybersecurity risk instead of relying on subjective ratings and gut feelings.
