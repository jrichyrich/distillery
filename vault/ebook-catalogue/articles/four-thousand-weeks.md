# Four Thousand Weeks: Time Management for Mortals
**Author:** Oliver Burkeman

## What This Book Is About
Named for the approximate number of weeks in a human lifespan, this book rejects the productivity culture's promise that you can fit everything in. Burkeman argues that embracing our radical finitude — accepting we'll never do it all — is paradoxically the path to a more meaningful and less anxious life. Part philosophy, part anti-productivity manifesto, it challenges readers to confront what really matters.

## Key Topics
- Embracing finitude and the limits of time
- Why productivity culture makes us more anxious, not less
- The trap of trying to optimize every moment
- Making peace with missing out
- Choosing meaningful commitments over endless efficiency

## When To Reach For This Book
When productivity optimization feels hollow and you need a philosophical reframe on what it means to spend a finite life well.
