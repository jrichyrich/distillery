# The Art of Thinking in Graphs
**Author:** Dolev Erez

## What This Book Is About
An illustrated guide to 52 psychological principles and cognitive phenomena that shape productivity, decision-making, and thinking. Each principle is presented with a visual graph, making abstract concepts like the Dunning-Kruger Effect, loss aversion, the Pareto Principle, and decision fatigue tangible and memorable.

## Key Topics
- Dunning-Kruger Effect and Imposter Syndrome
- Flow state and compound interest
- Loss aversion and sunk cost fallacy
- The Pareto Principle (80/20 rule)
- Decision fatigue and Hick's Law
- Hawthorne Effect, Streisand Effect, and other cognitive biases
- The forgetting curve and Gaussian distribution

## When To Reach For This Book
When you want a quick visual reference for the most important mental models and cognitive biases that affect your thinking and decisions.
