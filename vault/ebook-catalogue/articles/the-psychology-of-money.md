# The Psychology of Money
**Author:** Morgan Housel

## What This Book Is About
The Psychology of Money explores the strange ways people think about money and how behavior matters more than knowledge when it comes to financial success. Through 19 short stories, Morgan Housel examines the emotional, psychological, and social forces that shape financial decisions -- from the power of compounding and the role of luck, to the difference between being wealthy and being rich. The book argues that doing well with money has little to do with intelligence and everything to do with behavior.

## Key Topics
- How personal history shapes financial behavior
- The power of compounding over time
- The role of luck and risk in financial outcomes
- The difference between getting wealthy and staying wealthy
- Saving, spending, and the illusion of control
- Tail events and their outsized impact on results

## When To Reach For This Book
When you want to understand the behavioral and emotional side of money management rather than just the technical mechanics of investing.
