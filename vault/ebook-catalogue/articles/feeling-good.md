# Feeling Good: The New Mood Therapy
**Author:** David D. Burns

## What This Book Is About
The pioneering book that brought cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) to the general public. Burns explains how distorted thinking patterns — not external events — cause depression and anxiety, then provides practical techniques to identify and correct these cognitive distortions. Clinically proven to be as effective as medication for many patients, this book has helped millions overcome depression without drugs.

## Key Topics
- The ten cognitive distortions that fuel depression
- Techniques for challenging and reframing negative thoughts
- Building self-esteem through cognitive restructuring
- Overcoming perfectionism, guilt, and approval addiction
- Dealing with criticism and interpersonal conflicts
- The relationship between thoughts, feelings, and behavior

## When To Reach For This Book
When you or someone you know is struggling with depression, anxiety, or persistently negative thinking and wants evidence-based self-help tools.
