# Good Strategy Bad Strategy
**Author:** Richard Rumelt

## What This Book Is About
Rumelt argues that most organizations confuse goals, slogans, and wishful thinking with actual strategy. A good strategy, he explains, has a "kernel" consisting of three elements: a diagnosis of the challenge, a guiding policy for dealing with it, and coherent actions designed to carry out that policy. The book uses compelling examples from business, military, and public policy to show the difference between truly powerful strategies and the fluff that passes for strategy in most organizations.

## Key Topics
- The kernel of good strategy: diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent action
- Common hallmarks of bad strategy (fluff, failure to face the challenge, mistaking goals for strategy)
- The power of proximate objectives
- Leveraging advantages and creating competitive positions
- Strategic thinking as problem-solving, not goal-setting

## When To Reach For This Book
When you need to develop a real strategy (not just goals) or want to diagnose why a current strategy isn't working.
