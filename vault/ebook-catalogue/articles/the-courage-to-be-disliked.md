# The Courage to Be Disliked
**Author:** Ichiro Kishimi & Fumitake Koga

## What This Book Is About
A Japanese bestseller presented as a Socratic dialogue between a philosopher and a young man, introducing Alfred Adler's psychology. The book argues that we are not determined by our past, that all problems are interpersonal, and that true happiness comes from having the courage to be disliked by others while living authentically according to your own values.

## Key Topics
- Adlerian psychology fundamentals
- Separation of tasks (whose problem is it?)
- Freedom from the desire for recognition
- Living in the present, not determined by the past
- Interpersonal relationship frameworks
- Community feeling and contribution to others

## When To Reach For This Book
When you feel trapped by others' expectations or approval-seeking behavior and want a philosophical framework for living more freely.
